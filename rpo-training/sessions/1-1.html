Session 1.1: Prompt Engineering 101 for Recruiters

Your guide to moving from simple questions to expert commands.

The #1 Skill for Working with AI

The goal today is to become an expert instructor for your AI assistant. In our survey, the #1 skill gap was knowing how to get the AI to do what you actually want.

Think of AI as the world's most brilliant junior assistant: it's incredibly fast and knowledgeable, but it has zero context about your job. A vague instruction gets a vague result. An expert instruction gets an expert result.

A Tale of Two Prompts

The "Before" Prompt

This is how most people start. The result is generic, boring, and won't attract top talent because the AI has to guess everything.

write a job description for a software engineer

The "After" Prompt (C.R.E.A.T.E.)

This is an expert command. It gives the AI a professional brief, not a vague question, leading to a high-quality result.

[CHARACTER] You are an expert RPO recruiter and recruitment marketer...
[REQUEST] Write a compelling job description for a "Senior Full-Stack Engineer" tailored to mid-market tech companies...
[EXAMPLES] Provide 3 short example bullets showing desired tone (concise, action-oriented, benefits-focused).
[ADDITIONS] Include location, required experience (5+ years), and must-have stacks (React, Node.js).
[TYPE] Output: Sections — Summary, Responsibilities, Required Skills, Nice-to-Have, Compensation Range, How to Apply.
[EXTRAS] Tone: professional & persuasive; length: ~300 words; avoid buzzwords.

The C.R.E.A.T.E. Framework

An expert prompt contains six key ingredients. Mastering this structure is the key to getting high-quality results every time.

Character

Who should the AI be?

Request

What action should it perform?

Examples

What does a good output look like?

Additions

Extra context, constraints, or details

Type

Format / structure of the response

Extras

Tone, length, and items to avoid

Lab Activities

Lab 1: Creating a Candidate Persona (15 Mins)

Your goal is to create a detailed candidate persona for a hard-to-fill "Senior Clinical Research Associate" role. If you don't know who you're looking for, you can't write content that resonates. Use the C.R.E.A.T.E. structure to build your prompt.

View Example Persona Prompt
[CHARACTER] You are a senior life sciences recruiter and candidate profiling expert...
[REQUEST] Create a detailed candidate persona for a "Senior Clinical Research Associate" based in Budapest...
[EXAMPLES] Provide sections and sample bullets (Summary, Demographics & Experience, Key Skills, Motivations, Where to Find Them).
[ADDITIONS] Include typical certifications, seniority level, and travel expectations.
[TYPE] Output: Headed sections with 3–5 bullets each.
[EXTRAS] Tone: factual and recruiter-facing; keep concise.

Lab 2: Drafting a Compelling Job Description (20 Mins)

Now, use the persona you just created to draft a job description that speaks directly to them. This time, include Examples showing tone & structure in the prompt so the AI replicates it.

View Example Job Description Prompt
[CHARACTER] You are an expert recruitment marketing copywriter...
[REQUEST] Write a full job description for a Senior CRA role targeting the persona we created...
[EXAMPLES] Include a short sample opening paragraph that demonstrates the desired tone: "Join us in a role that advances clinical innovation..."
[ADDITIONS] Include location, salary band (EUR mid-market), and required qualifications.
[TYPE] Structure the output with headings: "Your Mission", "A Day in the Life", "Required Skills", "Benefits".
[EXTRAS] Tone: confident & inclusive; length: ~350 words; avoid buzzwords like "rockstar".

Wrap-Up & Next Steps

Your new superpower is the C.R.E.A.T.E. Framework. Remember the golden rule: Vague Instructions = Vague Results. Expert Instructions = Expert Results.
