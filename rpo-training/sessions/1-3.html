Success Spotlight #1

A Real-World Win from Anna

The Problem: Anna was starting a search for a "Head of Digital Marketing" in a competitive fintech market. The old way involved hours of manual research on LinkedIn to understand the landscape.

The New Way: She built one powerful prompt to do the work for her using the C.R.E.A.T.E. framework.

Anna's Winning "Talent Landscape Snapshot" Prompt (C.R.E.A.T.E.)

[CHARACTER] You are a senior market intelligence analyst for a top executive search firm, specializing in the European fintech sector.
[REQUEST] Create a "Talent Landscape Snapshot" for the "Head of Digital Marketing" role for a fast-growing fintech startup in Budapest.
[EXAMPLES] Show one short example of the desired heading structure and an example bullet for "Target Companies" (e.g., "Company A — scale-up with strong performance marketing").
[ADDITIONS] Include company size, market segment, and 2–3 likely senior job titles that compete for this hire.
[TYPE] Format the output with headings: "Target Companies," "Common Job Titles," and "Key Skills & Technologies" — each with 3–8 bullets.
[EXTRAS] Keep concise, recruiter-facing tone; avoid speculative claims; return sources or rationale where possible.

The Impact: From Hours to Seconds

  • The AI provided a perfectly formatted report in ~30 seconds.
  • It identified target companies and more specific job titles like "VP of Growth."
  • It listed key technologies like "HubSpot" and "Salesforce Marketing Cloud."
  • This saved at least 1 hour of manual research and gave her a huge confidence boost.

Clinic Case Study: Peter's Problem

The Challenge: Peter wanted help writing screening questions for a "Junior Accountant" role, but his results were too generic.

Peter's "Before" Prompt:

Give me interview questions for a junior accountant.

The Group's "After" Prompt (C.R.E.A.T.E.):

[CHARACTER] You are a hiring manager experienced in screening entry-level finance candidates.
[REQUEST] Generate interview questions for a "Junior Accountant".
[EXAMPLES] Provide examples of behavioral and technical questions (e.g., "Tell me about a time you found and fixed a spreadsheet error").
[ADDITIONS] Key qualifications: attention to detail, Excel pivot tables experience, eagerness to learn; indicate desired seniority level and typical responsibilities.
[TYPE] Organize questions into categories: "Attention to Detail," "Excel Skills," and "Learning Agility" with 4–6 questions per category.
[EXTRAS] Include one suggested scoring rubric per category (1–5), and keep language concise and interviewer-friendly.

The Result: From Generic to Perfectly Structured

By adding Character, Additions (context) and Type (format), the AI produced specific, behavioral questions organized into the exact categories needed for a structured screening call. Peter got a ready-to-use list in minutes.
